The Sun

  • The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star (G2V) at the center of our solar system.
  • It is about 4.6 billion years old and contains 99.86% of the solar system’s mass.
  • The Sun's energy is produced through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium.
  • It has a surface temperature of around 5,500°C (9,932°F).

Neptune

  • Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the most distant in our solar system.
  • It has a deep blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.
  • Neptune is known for having the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).
  • It has 14 known moons, the largest being Triton.

Uranus

  • Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and has a pale blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.
  • It rotates on its side, making it unique among the planets.
  • Uranus has a ring system, though less prominent than Saturn's, and 27 known moons.
  • The average temperature on Uranus is -224°C (-371°F), making it one of the coldest planets.

Earth

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet known to support life.
  • It has a diverse climate system, ranging from polar ice caps to tropical rainforests.
  • About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, with land making up the rest.
  • Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon, which influences tides and stabilizes Earth's axial tilt.

Mars

  • Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide on its surface.
  • It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, which is about three times the height of Mount Everest.
  • Mars has polar ice caps made of both water and dry ice (frozen carbon dioxide).
  • Mars is a prime candidate for human exploration due to its similarities to Earth, despite being cold and having a thin atmosphere.

Venus

  • Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is similar in size and structure to Earth.
  • It has a thick atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide, which causes a runaway greenhouse effect.
  • Surface temperatures can reach 467°C (872°F), making Venus the hottest planet in the solar system.
  • Venus has a day longer than its year; it takes 243 Earth days to rotate once but only 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun.

Saturn

  • Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is best known for its stunning ring system.
  • Its rings are made mostly of ice and rock particles, some of which are as small as a grain of sand.
  • Saturn is a gas giant, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.
  • It has 83 known moons, including Titan, which has a thick atmosphere and liquid methane lakes.

Jupiter

  • Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, with a mass over 300 times that of Earth.
  • It has a famous storm called the Great Red Spot, which has been raging for over 300 years.
  • Jupiter is a gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • It has 95 known moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system.

Mercury

  • Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in our solar system.
  • It has no atmosphere to retain heat, causing extreme temperature variations between day and night.
  • Mercury has many craters due to its lack of an atmosphere to protect it from meteoroid impacts.
  • A year on Mercury lasts just 88 Earth days, but its day is the equivalent of 176 Earth days.
